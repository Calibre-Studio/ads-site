Not keywords. The conversation itself.

On Google you bid on words. On Meta you guess at interests. In ChatGPT you target context hints: plain-language descriptions of the situation your buyer is actually in. Someone mid-decision, describing their exact problem, is a different quality of intent than anything search or social can offer.

// Questions

FAQ

How is this different from Google or Meta ads?
No keywords, no interest profiles. You target context hints, plain-language descriptions of the situation your buyer is in. The ad appears mid-conversation, as a clearly-labelled sponsored card below ChatGPT's answer. It doesn't change what ChatGPT says.
Who actually sees ChatGPT ads?
Users on ChatGPT's Free and Go tiers. The Go tier ($8/mo) skews toward founders, freelancers and small-business decision-makers, people with real buying authority, which is part of why early performance data is strong for considered purchases.
Can you run this from Australia for our UK or US business?
Yes. We run a verified account that can target every open market: the US, UK, Canada, Australia and New Zealand. Targeting is country-level for now. The EU isn't open yet.
How do you measure results?
A browser pixel plus the server-side Conversions API (which survives ad blockers and cookie limits), UTMs, and a dedicated analytics view. Because a lot of ChatGPT-driven conversions arrive later via branded search or direct, we watch those during campaigns too.
What does a ChatGPT ad look like?
A "chat_card" below the response: a short title, a line of body copy, an image and a link, clearly marked Sponsored. We write and design it for the conversation it appears in.
Should we move our Google or Meta budget to it?
No. It's a new layer that reaches people earlier in their research, while your existing channels catch them at the moment of action. We run it alongside, measured together.
